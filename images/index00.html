Welcome to Classic Cars, a site dedicated to era of the classic automobile, and to its influence on modern culture.

The Classic Car

Ever since Karl Benz produced an automobile based on a four-stroke, gasoline-powered internal combustion engine in 1885, mankind has been facinated and seduced by the automobile.

It was, however, the introduction of the Model T Ford by Henry Ford in 1908 which revolutionized the car industry and brought motoring to the world, and beckoned in the era of the automobile.

Since then the car has changed our lives, changed our economies, and changed the world in which we live.

There is no single definition of a Classic Car, and it is probably wrong to be too rigid.

However, we consider a classic to be a car of great beauty, of great power, and of great majesty, which has stood the test of time, and which has enhanced the lives of its drivers.

In short, a classic car is a work of art.

Classic Cars in the Movies

It is arguably through the medium of the motion picture that the car has had the most profound influence on modern culture.

From Herbie in The Love Bug to the Aston Martin in Goldfinger, cars in the movies have influenced the way we think.

However, the single biggest influence, and the most enduring, must be the car chase.

Steve McQueen in Bullitt, Gene Hackman in The French Connection, and Dan Akroyd & John Belushi in the Blues Brothers, are just a few classic examples. These ideas are all discussed in our Movies page.

More about Classic Cars
