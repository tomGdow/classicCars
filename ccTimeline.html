Key Developments in the History of the Automobile

  • 1870 The Austrian Siegfried Marcus attached a petrol-powered internal combustion engine to what was once a horse-cart.
  • 1889 Gottlieb Daimler designs an automobile from from first principles, instead of modifying a horse-drawn vehicle.
  • 1885 Karl Benz and Gottlieb Daimler introduce their improved fourstroke internal combustion engine. By 1900, commercial production of automobiles is taking place in Germany and (under licence from Benz) in Paris.
  • 1901 The shock absorber is introduced by C.L. Horock.
  • 1908 William C Durant founds General Motors.
  • 1908 Henry Ford introduces the Model T Ford, which revolutionizes the motor industry. Assembly line production allows cars to be produced cheaply, thus making cars affordable to the general public for the first time. Until now, cars were only for the rich, and had no roof or windows.
  • The Dogde Motor Company, based in Michigen, USA, introduces the first car body made entirely of steel.
  • 1911 Ford Motor Company introduces the first moving assembly line.
  • 1926 Francis Wright Davis delelops power steering.
  • 1934 In France, Citroën develop the first mass-produced frond-drive car.
  • 1950s Cruise control is introduced, following observations and experiments by Ralph Teeter.
  • 1966 Fuel injection systems introduced.
  • 1970s Airbags become standard.
  • 1980s Fuel costs escalate.
  • 1985 Anti-locking breaks introduced.
